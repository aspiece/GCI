    Key Ideas You Can Use in Your Responses

    Use these core ideas from both transcripts when writing your responses.

    Video/Transcript 1: Accountability

    • "Do your job" can be right in high-pressure moments.
    • In collaboration, teams also need to find gaps and close them.
    • Ultimate accountability is to the outcome, not just individual tasks.
    • Strong teams define values, vision, mission, and then "find a way."

    Video/Transcript 2: Ropes Course Reflection

    • Course goals emphasize communication, cooperation, trust, and teamwork.
    • Students grow when they move from "I can't do that" to "I can do this."
    • Belay safety depends on multiple roles working together as one system.
    • The trapeze jump requires decision-making, courage, and trust in teammates.

    How This Assignment Helps You Learn

    This assignment is structured around a real team problem and follows Merrill's model:

    Problem-Centered

    Solve a real challenge: how to improve teamwork outcomes under pressure in class and future work settings.

    Activation

    Recall prior teamwork experiences and connect them to the transcript evidence bank before writing.

    Demonstration

    Analyze modeled teamwork from the required video plus both transcripts (accountability and ropes-course reflection).

    Application

    Apply concepts through written analysis, evidence-based responses, and a practical team protocol.

    Integration

    Transfer learning by setting a SMART goal and explaining how skills will be used in future teams.
