Day 1 – Intro to How 3D Printing Works

Intro & Setup

Goal: Build a strong foundation of how 3D printing works, then connect that knowledge to the Bambu A1 printer you will use in class.

Objectives

  • Explain additive vs. subtractive manufacturing in your own words (Evidence: Video Connection prompt #1).
  • Identify major Bambu printer components and describe each part’s function (Evidence: Component Hunt + Printer Anatomy table).
  • Describe how a digital model is sliced and printed layer by layer (Evidence: Video Connection prompts #2 and #3).
  • Document observations and reflections clearly (Evidence: Demo Observation Log + Exit Ticket).

Steps

  1. (8 min) Watch the How 3D Printers Work warm-up video first.
  2. (10 min) Complete the timed Component Hunt (3–5 minutes), then identify key parts (nozzle, bed, AMS, touchscreen, frame/gantry). Use the Bambu Academy Components Reference and AMS Guide while locating parts.
  3. (10 min) Observe a demonstration print, then review spool loading with the Load a Spool Guide.
  4. (17 min) Complete the Day 1 worksheet, then finish the Beginner Bambu Lab course module. Finally, click Copy Worksheet Notes and paste into your Digital Notebook Day 1 entry.

Optional Supports

Deliverable: Completed worksheet and labeled photo of printer setup.

Day 1 Worksheet

Guidance: Use complete sentences. Try to include one safety detail and one real observation from class in your answers.

Word Bank (Support)

  • Additive: building by adding material layer by layer
  • Subtractive: shaping by removing material
  • Slicing: splitting a digital model into printable layers
  • Nozzle: heated tip that extrudes filament
  • AMS: system that manages filament feeding/material options
  • Gantry: frame/motion system guiding print head movement

Day 2 – Design Your First Model

Design

Goal: Create a custom 3D model using Tinkercad and prepare it for slicing.

Steps

  1. Go to Tinkercad.com and log in with your school Google account.
  2. Create a name tag or keychain (3 mm thick, text raised at least 1 mm).
  3. Export your model as an STL file.
  4. Upload your STL to your class folder or assignment in Google Classroom.

Day 3 – Slice and Start Your Print

Slicing

Goal: Learn slicing basics and prepare your design for printing in Bambu Studio.

Start Here

Step 1: Watch this slicer software walkthrough before opening Bambu Studio.

Steps

  1. Open Bambu Studio.
  2. Import your STL file.
  3. Adjust settings: Layer Height 0.2 mm, Infill 15%, No Supports.
  4. Preview the print layers and save your 3MF file.
  5. Upload your 3MF file for teacher review before printing.

Resources

Day 4 – Troubleshooting and Reflection

Quality Check

Goal: Analyze your print results and document any issues or improvements.

Steps

  1. Carefully remove your print from the bed.
  2. Inspect the surface quality, edges, and lettering.
  3. Take a photo of your print.
  4. Complete the Print Log Reflection.

Capstone
