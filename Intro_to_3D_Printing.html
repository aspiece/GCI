Day 1 – Getting to Know the Bambu A1

Intro & Setup

Goal: Understand how the Bambu A1 works and complete your first demo print.

Steps

  1. Watch the Bambu A1 overview video.
  2. Identify key parts of the printer (nozzle, bed, AMS, touchscreen).
  3. Observe the demonstration print in action.
  4. Complete the Printer Anatomy & Reflection worksheet.

Day 2 – Design Your First Model

Design

Goal: Create a custom 3D model using Tinkercad and prepare it for slicing.

Steps

  1. Go to Tinkercad.com and log in with your school Google account.
  2. Create a name tag or keychain (3 mm thick, text raised at least 1 mm).
  3. Export your model as an STL file.
  4. Upload your STL to your class folder or assignment in Google Classroom.

Resources

  • Tinkercad Tutorial
  • How to Make a Keychain in Tinkercad
  • Example Keychain STL File
  • Google Classroom Assignment

Deliverable: STL file upload and screenshot of your model.

Day 3 – Slice and Start Your Print

Slicing

Goal: Learn slicing basics and prepare your design for printing in Bambu Studio.

Steps

  1. Open Bambu Studio.
  2. Import your STL file.
  3. Adjust settings: Layer Height 0.2 mm, Infill 15%, No Supports.
  4. Preview the print layers and save your 3MF file.
  5. Upload your 3MF file for teacher review before printing.

Resources

  • Bambu Studio Software
  • Slicing Tutorial Video
  • Example Project 3MF File

Day 4 – Troubleshooting and Reflection

Quality Check

Goal: Analyze your print results and document any issues or improvements.

Steps

  1. Carefully remove your print from the bed.
  2. Inspect the surface quality, edges, and lettering.
  3. Take a photo of your print.
  4. Complete the Print Log Reflection.

Resources

Day 5 – Remix and Improve

Capstone

Goal: Apply your learning by remixing or improving your model.

Steps

  1. Choose one: remix a model from Printables.com or improve your previous design.
  2. Modify or redesign in Tinkercad.
  3. Export the final STL file and take a screenshot of your new model.
  4. Write a short paragraph about what you learned and submit both files.
