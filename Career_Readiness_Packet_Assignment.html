Part 1: Starter

Project Scenario and Expectations

You are preparing a real-world job application packet for a position you are genuinely interested in.

Step 1: Choose a Job Posting First

  • Find one internship or entry-level job posting you want to target.
  • Copy/save the posting link and identify key skills, qualifications, and keywords.
  • Use that posting to tailor your resume and cover letter in the next sections.

Project Expectations

  • Create one polished 2-page packet: resume (page 1) and cover letter (page 2).
  • Tailor both documents to your selected posting.
  • Submit both `.docx` and `.pdf` versions of your final packet.

Estimated time: 45 minutes total.

  • Clear, concise writing with correct spelling and grammar.

Cover Letter Example (Fictional)

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am excited to apply for the Customer Support Intern position at BrightPath Solutions. In my student tech help desk role, I support over 40 students and staff with account and device issues while maintaining clear communication and accurate documentation. I am especially interested in this role because your posting emphasizes customer communication, troubleshooting, and teamwork—areas I use every week.

Your posting highlights the need for someone who can “work effectively in a fast-paced team environment” and “communicate clearly with users.” Through event support and peer tech assistance, I have built those exact skills while staying calm under pressure and solving problems quickly. For example, during our school technology rollout, I helped triage login problems, documented recurring issues, and shared patterns with staff so we could prevent repeat errors.

In addition to technical support, I bring strong organization and follow-through. As a school event team member, I helped coordinate check-in for a 120-student event, which required accurate tracking, clear directions for participants, and consistent communication across our team. I would bring the same reliability, customer-first mindset, and attention to detail to BrightPath Solutions.

I would welcome the opportunity to bring my organization, communication, and service mindset to your team. Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to discussing next steps.

Sincerely,
Maya Johnson

Rubric Summary (50 points)

Each category is scored on four levels: Exceeds (9–10), Meets (7–8), Developing (5–6), Beginning (0–4).

Structure & Completeness (10 pts)
  • Exceeds (9–10): All required sections are complete, targeted to the selected job, and clearly organized across the 2-page packet.
  • Meets (7–8): Most required sections are complete and appropriately organized with minor gaps.
  • Developing (5–6): Some required sections are missing, thin, or not clearly aligned to the posting.
  • Beginning (0–4): Major sections are missing or the packet does not follow the assignment structure.
Word Skills & Formatting (10 pts)
  • Exceeds (9–10): Clean, ATS-friendly formatting with consistent fonts, spacing, headings, and strong visual polish.
  • Meets (7–8): Mostly consistent formatting with only small issues that do not hurt readability.
  • Developing (5–6): Inconsistent spacing/font choices or formatting that occasionally reduces clarity.
  • Beginning (0–4): Formatting is difficult to read or includes multiple ATS-unfriendly choices (heavy graphics/tables/columns).
Professional Writing (10 pts)
  • Exceeds (9–10): Writing is specific, professional, role-focused, and mostly error-free with strong evidence of fit.
  • Meets (7–8): Writing is clear and mostly professional with minor grammar or specificity issues.
  • Developing (5–6): Writing is somewhat generic or repetitive with noticeable grammar/mechanics issues.
  • Beginning (0–4): Writing lacks professionalism, clarity, or relevance to the selected role.
Accessibility & Usability (10 pts)
  • Exceeds (9–10): Strong readability and accessibility choices are applied consistently throughout the packet.
  • Meets (7–8): Accessibility/readability is mostly strong with minor opportunities to improve.
  • Developing (5–6): Some readability or accessibility issues interfere with ease of use.
  • Beginning (0–4): Accessibility and readability needs are largely not addressed.
Submission Quality (10 pts)
  • Exceeds (9–10): Both required file types are complete, correctly named, and submission-ready.
  • Meets (7–8): Required files are submitted with minor naming or completeness issues.
  • Developing (5–6): One required file type is missing or files are only partially complete.
  • Beginning (0–4): Submission is incomplete, incorrectly formatted, or not aligned to requirements.
